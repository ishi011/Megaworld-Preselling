Metro Manila Subway Project

The Metro Manila Subway is a 33.1km underground rail project currently being built in Metro Manila, Philippines, linking Valenzuela with Ninoy Aquino International Airport through 17 stations and a depot. As the nation's first underground mass transit system, it is designed to handle up to 1.5 million passengers daily, with an initial capacity of 400,000 to 800,000 passengers. Initiated by the Department of Transportation (DOTr) and approved by the National Economic Development Authority (NEDA), the project is part of the Duterte Administration's Build, Build, Build initiative. The subway’s groundbreaking occurred in February 2019, with phase one expected to be finished by 2025 and the entire project slated for completion by July 2027.

Kalayaan Bridge also known as the BGC-Ortigas Link Bridge

The Kalayaan Bridge also known as the BGC-Ortigas Link Bridge, was complete on June 12, 2021. Lawton Avenue and Kapitolyo are connected by this four-lane, two-way bridge that traverses the Pasig River. Its primary objective is to reduce the travel time between Fort Bonifacio and Ortigas to a mere 12 minutes by alleviating traffic congestion on main roadways such as EDSA and C-5. The bridge not only alleviates traffic congestion but also improves connectivity between critical business districts, thereby stimulating economic growth and development in the surrounding region. The enhanced accessibility has also made it more convenient for residents and commuters, contributing to a more efficient transportation network in Metro Manila.

Expansion Of Lawton Avenue

The expansion of Lawton Avenue, which is part of the infrastructure improvements in Bonifacio Global City (BGC), has indeed been a significant development. This project expanded the road from four lanes to six lanes, covering the stretch from 5th Avenue to Bayani Road. This widening project has notably eased traffic congestion along major thoroughfares such as EDSA, South Superhighway, and C-5 Road. Additionally, it has reduced the travel time from Bonifacio Global City to Ninoy Aquino International Airport to approximately 15 minutes12. This expansion is part of a broader effort to improve connectivity and accessibility within the area, complementing other infrastructure projects like the Kalayaan Bridge

New Senate Of The Philippines

The New Senate of the Philippines was built on a 20,000-sqm (2-hectare) site owned by the Bases Conversion and Development Authority (BCDA), which lies in the Navy Village at Fort Bonifacio. Such a prime location is situated in the rapidly rising Bonifacio Global City (BGC), noted for its modern infrastructure and vibrant business environment. Designed with historical importance but also modernity, it becomes a landmark for good governance. It stands surrounded by high-end housing estates as well as places of culture, signalling our country’s aim to be modernizing and progressive. The establishment of this new facility promises to improve the legislative space while at the same time promoting dynamic growth patterns in BGC.

Skytrain By Infracorp

The Skytrain project by Infracorp, a subsidiary of Alliance Global Group Inc., is an exciting development for the Bonifacio Global City (BGC) area. This P3.3-billion project aims to utilize automated cable-propelled monorail technology to significantly reduce travel time between Uptown BGC and MRT Guadalupe in Makati to just five minutes12. The Skytrain is expected to benefit around 60,000 to 100,000 passengers daily, making commuting more efficient and convenient12. This project is part of the broader efforts to improve transportation infrastructure in Metro Manila, enhancing connectivity and reducing traffic congestion3.
